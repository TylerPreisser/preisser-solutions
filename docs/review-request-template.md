# Review Request Template — Google Reviews

Send this after a successful project delivery, ideally within 7-14 days of go-live when results are visible but momentum is fresh. Goal: organic, natural-sounding reviews that surface the language real prospects type into Google and AI tools.

**Channel:** email first (highest reply rate), follow up with SMS / call only if no reply after 5-7 days.

**Direct review link to use everywhere:** `https://g.page/r/{GBP_REVIEW_LINK_ID}/review` — replace `{GBP_REVIEW_LINK_ID}` with the actual GBP short link once GBP is claimed.

---

## Template A — Standard (use 80% of the time)

**Subject:** Quick favor — would you write a Google review?

Hi {{first_name}},

It's been great working with you on {{project_name}}. I'm really glad the {{specific_thing_built}} is doing what we hoped — {{specific_outcome_so_far}}.

If you have two minutes and you're happy with how it turned out, would you leave a quick Google review? It genuinely helps the next Kansas business owner who's trying to figure out whether to trust a small Hays-based shop with their {{automation/website/dashboard/CRM/AI}} work.

Here's the direct link:
{{review_link}}

A few honest sentences in your own words is all I'm after — what you came in needing, what we built, and how it's going. No template, no checklist. Whatever feels natural.

Thanks for the trust on this one.

Tyler
Preisser Solutions

## What to encourage (organically)

These are the words and phrases that match what prospects actually search for and that LLMs use to decide what Preisser Solutions does. **Do not list these to the client.** Just let the project context surface them naturally — the result-led template above already steers in this direction.

- **What we build:** website, web application, dashboard, CRM, AI agent, automation, integration, custom software, internal tool
- **Specialties:** local SEO, AI search optimization, business process automation, lead follow-up, customer reactivation, invoicing automation, inventory management
- **Place:** Hays, Kansas, western Kansas, Ellis County, Fort Hays State University
- **Mode:** founder-led, no subcontractors, custom-coded, fixed-price, in-person, fast turnaround, direct access to Tyler
- **Outcome words:** speed, results, ROI, time saved, reactivated customers, faster scheduling, better dashboard, easier reporting

A natural review that mentions even three of these in plain English is worth more than a polished one written by an SEO person.

---

## What NOT to do

- Don't write the review for the client (against Google policy — gets removed).
- Don't offer discounts, gifts, or referral fees in exchange for a review (against Google policy + Federal Trade Commission rules).
- Don't ask for "5 stars." Ask for an honest review.
- Don't follow up more than twice. If they don't reply after Template C, drop it and ask the next client.
- Don't send to clients who had a rough engagement. Fix the relationship first, ask later (or never).

# Preisser Tech — Researched Starting Prices

**Date:** 2026-05-03
**Researcher:** Internal pricing analyst
**Subject:** Defensible "starting at" prices for the 5 Preisser Tech service lines

---

## Methodology

I sampled three concentric circles of comparable supply: (1) Kansas-local boutique web/IT shops named in Tyler's competitive set (Lifted Logic KCMO, Active Logic KC, Vorgestern Manhattan KS, Web Design KC, Cassandra Bryan Wichita, MKS Manhattan, Lost Highway Hays, Pluto Sites Hays, Adams Brown Wichita), (2) national boutique custom-code firms working in the same Next.js/React/TypeScript stack (Cocoon, Lucky Media, Black Airplane, Postlight tier), and (3) industry-wide pricing surveys for AI agents, business automation, custom CRMs, and BI dashboards (Clutch, DesignRush, AgixTech, Aplos AI, Cleveroad, Tableau/Power BI consulting market reports). Where competitors do not publish prices, I triangulated from published case-study scope, Clutch "minimum project size" filters, and the cost-calculator inputs the agency itself uses. Every recommended number below is anchored to at least three independent data points and positioned in the **top 25–40% of the observed market** — premium, but below enterprise-team pricing.

## Strategic positioning notes

### On round vs. precise numbers
**Use precise numbers, not round ones.** $12,500 / $28,500 / $9,500 outperform $10,000 / $25,000 / $10,000 for three reasons:

1. **Round numbers signal "package," precise numbers signal "scoped engagement."** A $10,000 starting price reads like a tier off a menu. $12,500 reads like the floor of a real estimate.
2. **Round numbers invite negotiation. Precise numbers don't.** "Can you do $9,500?" is a much harder ask against a $12,500 list price than against a $10,000 list price — the precise number implies a scoping process behind it.
3. **Round numbers are what mid-market competitors use** (Web Design KC: $9,400 is borderline, but Lifted Logic's calculator and Vorgestern's $5K–$25K are both round). Differentiating on number-shape alone is a free signaling win.

The **two exceptions** where round is fine: **$8,500** (automation) and **$15,000** (AI agents). $8,500 because it sits clearly between the $5K and $10K psychological brackets and the precision is already baked in. $15,000 because at the AI-agent price point the round number actually communicates **confidence** — "we don't haggle on AI work; this is the floor" — and the next tier ($25K–$40K) is where real customization begins.

### On psychological thresholds
**Cross the $10K Rubicon on Custom Websites and Web Apps.** Both should start above $10K. Clutch data and every market guide bifurcates at $10K — under $10K is "template-shop territory," over $10K is "custom engineering." The $12,500 website price does this cleanly. Anything starting with "9,...." would put Preisser Tech mentally in the same bin as Toucan and Pluto Sites.

**Stay below the $30K wall on Web Apps.** $28,500 is intentional — it stays under the $30K threshold where buyers start expecting a multi-person team, project manager, dedicated QA, and SOC-2 documentation. Tyler is one person; pricing above $30K invites comparisons he loses to Active Logic. $28,500 says "premium solo founder," $35,000 says "where's your team."

**$15K is the right "filter" price for AI Agents.** Anyone who balks at $15K for an AI agent is not Tyler's customer — they want a Fiverr GPT wrapper. Anyone who nods at $15K is pre-qualified for the $25K–$40K real engagement that follows. This is the single most important brand-protecting number on the list.

### How these prices feel against the brand
Read together — **$12,500 / $28,500 / $8,500 / $15,000 / $9,500** — these prices do three things:

1. **They are unmistakably above every Kansas-local solo shop** (Toucan, Pluto Sites, Lost Highway, MKS, Web Design KC Signature). No accidental head-to-head with bargain templates.
2. **They are visibly below KC enterprise-agency pricing** (Lifted Logic $50K avg, Active Logic $50K floor). Tyler is not pretending to be a 12-person agency; he is the premium solo option.
3. **They land at the bottom of the national premium boutique band** ($30K–$80K Next.js MVP territory) — meaning Tyler can credibly serve a Kansas City or national client who has shopped Cocoon or Lucky Media and found the $50K floor too high.

### Tire-kicker filters
The two prices that **intentionally filter** are **$15,000 (AI Agents)** and **$28,500 (Web Apps)**. Tyler should not lower either. The website ($12,500), automation ($8,500), and dashboard ($9,500) prices are intentionally **accessible** entry points designed to convert a curious local SMB into a real client — and to upsell from there. The AI Agent and Web App prices are designed to keep Preisser Tech out of conversations it would lose on price.

### One last note on display
On the website, present these as: **"Starting at $12,500"** — never "$12,500 packages" or "$12,500 fixed price." The "starting at" language is doing crucial work — it tells buyers this is a floor, not a cap, and that real engagements are scoped from there. Pair every starting price with a one-line case-study anchor ("HG Oil inventory system" beside the Web App price; "Cassidy HVAC reactivation engine" beside Automation; etc.) so the number is never naked.
